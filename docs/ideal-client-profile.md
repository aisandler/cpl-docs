## ðŸ“Š ICP Breakdown by Segment

### **Segment 1: Commercial Referral Sources (PRIMARY - 35% of Marketing)**

#### **WHO THEY ARE:**

**Commercial Real Estate Brokers:**
- **Large National:** CBRE, JLL, Cushman & Wakefield, Newmark
- **Mid-Size Specialists:** Avison Young, Colliers, Savills
- **Boutique Commercial:** 5-20 broker firms, specialized markets
- **Role:** Senior brokers, managing directors, partners

**Commercial Lenders:**
- **National Banks:** Chase, BofA, Wells Fargo
- **Regional Banks:** Signature Bank, Valley National, Sterling
- **Mortgage Brokers:** Independent commercial mortgage advisors
- **Private Lenders:** Debt funds, family offices, alternative lenders

**Leasing Agents:**
- Landlord representatives (marketing space)
- Tenant representatives (finding space for businesses)
- Both office and retail specialists

#### **DEMOGRAPHICS:**
- **Age:** 35-65 (established professionals)
- **Experience:** 10-30+ years in commercial real estate
- **Location:** NYC (Manhattan, Brooklyn), Tri-state region
- **Income:** $150K-$500K+ (commission-based)

#### **FIRMOGRAPHICS:**
- **Company Size:** 5-500+ employees (from boutiques to nationals)
- **Deal Volume:** 10-100+ transactions per year
- **Deal Size:** $2M-$100M+ (CPL sweet spot: $2M-$20M)
- **Geographic Focus:** NYC primary, national for lending

#### **PSYCHOGRAPHICS:**

**Values:**
- Reliability over flash (need attorneys who close deals)
- Responsiveness (deals move fast, need immediate answers)
- Professionalism without arrogance ("big ego" attitude turns them off)
- Broker-friendly culture (understand their role, respect referrals)

**Pain Points:**
- **Big firms:** Sophisticated but slow, expensive, institutional barriers, "big ego" attitude
- **Small firms:** Accessible but lack sophistication for complex deals
- **Need:** Both sophistication AND service (CPL's "Service Sophistication Hybrid")

**Buying Behavior:**
- Relationship-driven (trust, track record, personal connection)
- Seek bank-approved list status (credibility signal)
- Value speed and responsiveness
- Appreciate proactive communication
- Want partners, not vendors

**Success Metrics:**
- Deal closes on time and smoothly
- Attorney doesn't create problems/delays
- Client (buyer/seller/borrower) is satisfied
- Broker looks good to their client
- Attorney is easy to work with again

#### **WHAT THEY NEED FROM CPL:**

**Primary Needs:**
1. Commercial sophistication (handle $2M-$100M deals)
2. Partner-level attention (not junior associates)
3. Bank-approved list status (credibility with lenders)
4. Speed and responsiveness (deals move fast)
5. Broker-friendly culture (understand their role)
6. Alan's CPA+Attorney credentials (for complex tax situations)

**Communication Preferences:**
- Quick phone calls (not long emails)
- Text for urgent matters
- Regular check-ins on deals in progress
- Proactive updates (don't wait for them to ask)

**Deal Types:**
- Commercial sales ($2M-$100M)
- Commercial lending (purchase, refi, construction)
- Commercial leasing (office, retail, industrial)
- 1031 exchanges (Alan's CPA expertise valuable)
- Investment properties, portfolio acquisitions
