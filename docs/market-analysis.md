**Implication:** Market skepticism about large firms' boutique claims creates opportunity for AUTHENTIC boutique firms like CPL.

**CPL Advantage:** Genuinely boutique-sized with authentic partner-level attention on every deal.

### **3. The "Specialization Gap"**

**Finding:** Competitors are either too specialized (limiting market) or too generalist (divided attention). Few occupy the "specialized generalist" position in real estate.

**Analysis:**

**Too Specialized (Narrow Market):**
- SSRGA: Co-op/condo BOARDS only (not buyers/sellers)
- AGMB: REBNY broker counsel (advisory, not transactional primary)
- SLPS: Transactional only (no litigation)

**Too Generalist (Divided Attention):**
- Meister Seelig: RE is 1 of 12 practices
- MMPS: RE is 1 of 10+ practices
- Falcon Rappaport: RE is 1 of 20+ practices
- AGMB: RE is 1 of 20+ practices
- Sandor Krauss: RE is 1 of 20+ practices

**Pure RE Specialists:**
- Rosenberg & Estis: 90+ attorneys (too large for many clients)
- BBG: 60 attorneys, commercial-centric
- Romer Debbas: 30+ attorneys, residential-centric volume mill
- SLPS: Small traditional boutique, transactional only

**CPL Sweet Spot:** 100% real estate focus BUT comprehensive (transactional + litigation, commercial + residential, full-service within RE specialty). Not one narrow niche, not divided across multiple practices.

**Finding:** Some competitors represent lenders/institutions (the "other side"), creating opportunity for client-first positioning.

**Lender/Institution Representatives:**

- **SSRGA:** Primarily represents co-op/condo BOARDS (not buyers/sellers)

**Client-Side Representatives:**
- Most others represent property buyers/sellers/owners

**Implication:** Many deals involve CPL representing CLIENTS against these firms representing banks or boards.

**CPL Positioning:** "Your Advocate" - we represent YOU (buyers, sellers, owners, developers), not banks or boards. When MMPS represents the lender or SSRGA represents the board, CPL represents YOU.
